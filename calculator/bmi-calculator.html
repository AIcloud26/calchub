How to Calculate BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight that applies to adult men and women.

BMI Formula (Metric)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)

Example: 70 kg ÷ (1.75 m)² = 22.86

BMI Formula (Imperial)

BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height² (in²)) × 703

Example: (154 lbs ÷ 70² in²) × 703 = 22.09

BMI Categories

Category BMI Range
Underweight Below 18.5
Normal weight 18.5 – 24.9
Overweight 25.0 – 29.9
Obese 30.0 and above

Frequently Asked Questions

A healthy BMI for most adults is between 18.5 and 24.9. However, BMI is a general indicator and may not be suitable for athletes, elderly individuals, or certain ethnic groups.

BMI has limitations. It doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass, doesn't account for fat distribution, and may not accurately reflect health status for athletes, elderly, pregnant women, or different ethnic groups.

BMI is just one indicator of health. Consult with a healthcare professional who can provide a comprehensive assessment considering your overall health, lifestyle, and medical history.

This calculator is designed for adults (age 20+). For children and teens, BMI is interpreted differently based on age and gender growth charts. Consult a pediatrician for proper assessment.
